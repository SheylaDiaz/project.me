Wonders about the ocean.

What do we know about the ocean?

The ocean, a vast and complex saltwater body, covers approximately 71% of Earth's surface and holds about 97% of its water. It's a crucial part of Earth's climate system, regulating temperatures and influencing weather patterns. The ocean is also a major source of oxygen and a home to a diverse range of marine life, according to the National Geographic Society.

How does the ocean help us?

It provides essential resources like food, oxygen, and fresh water, regulates climate, and supports economic activities.

How can we help the ocean?

We can reduce our carbon footprint, minimize plastic use, choose sustainable seafood, and conserve water.

How are humans hurting the ocean?

Humans impact the ocean through various activities, including pollution, overfishing, climate change, habitat destruction, and introducing invasive species. These actions disrupt marine ecosystems, harm marine life, and threaten the ocean's health and biodiversity.

Cause Impact
Pollution Contaminates water, harms marine life, and disrupts ecosystems.
Overfishing Depletes fish populations and affects food chains.
Climate Change Warms ocean temperatures, causing coral bleaching and sea level rise.
Habitat Destruction Destroys vital marine habitats like coral reefs and mangroves.
Invasive Species Alters the balance of native ecosystems and threatens biodiversity.

How many different species are in the ocean?

Scientists estimate there are over 230,000 known marine species, but the actual number may be over 2 million.

How many endangered species are there in the ocean?

Over 2,000 marine species are currently listed as endangered or vulnerable.

How many rare species are in the ocean?

Rare marine species are hard to track, but thousands exist, many still undiscovered or under-researched.
